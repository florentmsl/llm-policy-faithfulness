### Goal recap (what the policy is trying to achieve)

The reward is **+1 only when Chicken1 reaches the top** (completes a crossing). So the learned policy should mainly try to **move UP as often as possible**, but **avoid getting hit** because collisions knock the chicken backward and waste time, reducing total crossings.

### What the policy does overall

This policy is a **cautious “advance when safe, otherwise pause or retreat” controller** for Chicken1.

Concretely:

* **Default behavior is to press UP.**
  Across most branches, the returned action is `1  # UP`. This matches the objective: keep making progress toward the top to score crossings.

* **It switches to DOWN when cars are too close.**
  Many decisions hinge on `ED(Car?, Chicken1)` (Euclidean distance) being below some threshold (often tens to ~150). When a car is detected as *near*, the policy often returns `2  # DOWN`, meaning it intentionally steps back a lane to avoid an imminent hit.

* **It sometimes NOOPs to “wait for a gap.”**
  Several branches return `0  # NOOP` when certain car–car spacing or car–chicken alignment conditions suggest traffic is about to clear. This is the “don’t rush into the lane yet” behavior: hold position briefly rather than advancing into danger.

* **It uses relative ordering and lane/axis-specific cues to decide safety.**
  The conditions are built from:

  * `LT(A, B).x / .y`: whether one object is “less than” another along x or y (a crude left/right or above/below check),
  * `D(A, B).x / .y`: signed separations on a single axis,
  * `C(A, B).x / .y`: comparisons that look like “is A close/aligned to B on this axis?”
  * and many `ED(...)` distance checks.

  Together, these act like **hand-crafted safety tests**: “is a vehicle near me in my lane region (y relation) and close laterally (x relation)?” If yes → **DOWN or wait**; if no → **UP**.

* **Chicken2 appears as a situational signal, not the objective.**
  Some branches check distances involving `Chicken2` (e.g., `ED(Chicken2, Car9)`), suggesting the policy uses the other chicken’s position as extra context about traffic timing or shared lane occupancy, but actions still aim to optimize **Chicken1’s** crossings.

### In plain terms

The agent mostly **pushes upward aggressively to score**, but when its feature tests indicate that one or more cars are **within a danger radius or about to intersect its path**, it will **hesitate (NOOP)** or **back up (DOWN)** to avoid being struck—then it resumes moving **UP** once the situation looks safer.
