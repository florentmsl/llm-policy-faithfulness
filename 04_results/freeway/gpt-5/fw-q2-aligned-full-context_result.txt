### Goal of the task

The chicken can only move **up** (forward), **down** (back), or **do nothing**. The objective is to **cross from bottom to top repeatedly** to score points, while **not getting hit** (because hits knock the chicken back).

### What the learned policy does overall

This policy is essentially a **cautious “advance unless a collision is imminent” controller**:

* **Default behavior: push UP often.**
  Across most branches, the action returned is **UP**, meaning the chicken generally tries to make steady forward progress toward the top.

* **Uses “danger proximity” checks to decide when to hesitate or retreat.**
  Many conditions are thresholds on distances like `ED(Car?, Chicken1)` (chicken-to-car closeness) and relative-position tests like `LT(...).x/.y` and `D(...).x/.y`. When these indicate certain cars are **too close** (small `ED`) or positioned in a way that suggests an imminent hit, the policy switches to:

  * **DOWN**: a short retreat to avoid entering/remaining in a dangerous lane.
  * **NOOP**: brief waiting, presumably to let a nearby car pass.

* **Reactive rather than planning far ahead.**
  The logic repeatedly checks distances to several individual cars (Car1, Car2, Car5, Car7, Car8, Car9, Car10 show up a lot). The pattern is:
  **if safe margins to key nearby cars → UP**, otherwise **DOWN/NOOP** until margins increase.

* **Occasional “oscillation” to avoid hits.**
  Because it sometimes chooses **DOWN** in response to close cars and returns to **UP** once distances exceed a threshold, it effectively performs **micro back-and-forth adjustments** (wait/step back, then surge forward) to thread through traffic gaps.

In short: **it tries to cross quickly by moving UP most of the time, but repeatedly checks nearby cars and will pause or step DOWN when those cars get too close, then resume moving UP once the gap is safer.**
