1. **Baseline behavior in the original environment (cars moving):**

   * The policy is **strongly biased toward `UP`**: most leaf nodes return `1  # UP`, so the chicken generally **keeps pushing forward** to cross lanes.
   * It uses lots of **distance-to-car** (`ED(...)`) and **relative position** tests (`LT(...).x/.y`, `D(...).x/.y`, `C(...).x`) as “danger heuristics.” When some cars are **very close** (small `ED` thresholds like ~8–40) or certain relative configurations trigger, it sometimes chooses **`DOWN` or `NOOP`**—acting like a **hesitation / retreat** when it “believes” a collision is likely.
   * Because the rule conditions depend heavily on **car/chicken relative geometry**, and cars’ **x-positions change over time**, the action can **flip quickly** as traffic passes: overall you’d see **mostly upward progress**, but with **occasional stutters (NOOP)** and **brief backing up (DOWN)** near moments where traffic is close, reducing efficiency vs. a pure “always-up” controller.

2. **Behavior in the simplified environment (all cars immobilized):**

   * With cars frozen, all those proximity/ordering checks become **much more static and predictable**. Since the chicken doesn’t move sideways, the main thing changing is the chicken’s **y relative to fixed cars**.
   * The policy will still **mostly output `UP`**, so the chicken will **generally march upward and keep scoring crossings**.
   * However, as it passes specific fixed car positions, some “close distance” conditions can still trigger **unnecessary `DOWN` or `NOOP`** (cautious reactions to being near a car even though no timing is needed). So you’d expect **slower-than-optimal crossings** (more pauses/backsteps), but **far fewer true failures**, and it should still reliably make it across repeatedly.
