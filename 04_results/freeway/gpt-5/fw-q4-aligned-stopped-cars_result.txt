1. **Baseline behavior in the original environment**  
The policy is a large, reactive rule tree that depends heavily on relative positions/distances to many cars (and even `Chicken2`), not just on “go up safely now.” In the normal Freeway setting (cars moving), those features change constantly, so branch decisions flip often.  
Expected behavior: the chicken is **upward-biased overall** (many leaves return `UP`), but it will also **frequently inject `DOWN` and `NOOP`** when certain distance thresholds trigger. So it likely moves up in bursts, then hesitates or backtracks, with inconsistent crossing efficiency and frequent disruption from traffic dynamics.

2. **Behavior in the simplified environment**  
With all cars immobilized, most car-x / car-car-x conditions become effectively fixed, so the tree follows a much more stable set of branches.  
Expected behavior: the policy becomes more consistent and mostly acts like a **repeated upward mover** with occasional fixed stalls/backsteps (`NOOP`/`DOWN`) at specific state thresholds. Because traffic no longer changes and timing gaps are no longer needed, it should cross more reliably than in baseline, though still not perfectly efficient due to unnecessary rule-triggered `DOWN`/`NOOP` actions.
