The policy is a reactive rule‑set that constantly checks how close the chicken is to the various cars in the ten lanes and whether any car is aligned vertically with the chicken.  
- It first tests whether the chicken is left of Car 1 (`LT(Chicken1,Car1).x ≤ 0`). If true, it proceeds through a cascade of checks that look at the horizontal gaps to Cars 2‑10, the vertical offsets of Cars 8‑10, and the Euclidean distances (`ED`) between the chicken and each car.  
- Whenever those gaps are larger than the hard‑coded thresholds (e.g., `ED(Car1,Chicken1) ≤ 79`, `C(Car6,Car2).x ≤ 91`, etc.), the policy returns **UP** (action 1), meaning it tries to advance the chicken toward the top of the road.  
- If any of those gaps fall below the thresholds – indicating a car is too close horizontally or is about to intersect the chicken’s vertical path – the policy returns **DOWN** (action 2) to retreat to a safer lane, or **NOOP** (action 0) when the chicken is already in a relatively safe spot and moving would risk a collision.  
- The same structure is mirrored for the case where the chicken is right of Car 1, giving symmetric logic for the opposite side of the road.

In short, the policy continuously evaluates the immediate proximity and alignment of surrounding cars and chooses to move forward when the lane ahead is clear, to step back when a car threatens to hit the chicken, and to stay still when neither action is needed. This yields a simple obstacle‑avoidance controller that repeatedly attempts to cross the freeway while retreating or waiting only when necessary to avoid collisions.
