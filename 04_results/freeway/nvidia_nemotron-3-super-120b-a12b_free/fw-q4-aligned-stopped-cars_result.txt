**Baseline (original Freeway with moving traffic)**  
The policy constantly checks the chicken’s horizontal and vertical relationships to each car (LT, D, ED, C predicates). When the chicken is well‑aligned with a gap and the nearest car ahead is far enough (large ED/D values), the tree returns **UP**, pushing the chicken toward the next lane. If a car is too close below or the horizontal offset indicates an imminent collision, the policy returns **DOWN** to retreat or stay clear. When the chicken is positioned in a gap but needs to wait for a car to pass laterally, the tree yields **NOOP**. Over the two‑minute episode this produces a pattern of steady upward progress punctuated by occasional downward corrections and brief pauses, allowing the chicken to weave through the moving traffic and score multiple crossings.

**Simplified (stop_all_cars_tunnel – cars frozen in a static tunnel)**  
With all cars halted, the spatial predicates become fixed functions of the chicken’s y‑position only (the x‑relations are constant because the cars are not moving). The decision tree therefore collapses to a set of y‑thresholds: for low y it mostly returns **UP**; as the chicken climbs, certain combinations of LT/ED/C tests flip, causing the policy to output **DOWN** or **NOOP** once the chicken reaches the vertical limits of the static gap. Consequently the chicken will ascend until it hits the y‑level where the policy switches to DOWN/NOOP, then either hold position or descend back to the lower region, creating a small limit‑cycle (e.g., ascend to a safe y, then descend, repeat). The chicken shuttles back and forth inside the tunnel formed by the stopped cars but does not complete a full bottom‑to‑top crossing because the policy has no explicit reset logic for reaching the top.
