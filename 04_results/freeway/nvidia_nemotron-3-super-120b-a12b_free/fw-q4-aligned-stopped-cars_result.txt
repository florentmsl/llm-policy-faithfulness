**Baseline (original Freeway with moving traffic)**  
The policy constantly evaluates the relative positions and distances of the chicken to each car (using LT, ED, D, and C predicates). When a car is approaching from ahead or the chicken is too close to a vehicle, the conditionals that check short Euclidean distances (e.g., `ED(Car1, Chicken1) ≤ 79`) or relative x‑positions (`LT(Chicken1, Car1).x ≤ 0`) become true, causing the policy to choose **DOWN** (or sometimes NOOP) to retreat or wait. When the chicken is safely ahead of or far enough from the vehicles (the distance tests fail and the x‑order tests indicate the chicken is ahead of the traffic), the policy selects **UP**. Consequently, the agent repeatedly waits for gaps in traffic, darts forward when a gap appears, and steps back when a vehicle threatens it. This yields a cautious but effective crossing pattern that allows the chicken to reach the top repeatedly within the two‑minute sixteen‑second limit, scoring multiple points.

**Behavior in the simplified environment (all cars immobilized)**  
Because the cars no longer move horizontally, their x‑positions and the Euclidean distances to the chicken change only as the chicken’s y‑coordinate changes. The static layout makes the truth values of all the LT, ED, D, and C tests settle into a fixed pattern after the first few steps. In this static setting, the distance‑based conditions that previously triggered a DOWN retreat (e.g., `ED(Car1, Chicken1) ≤ 79`) are either never satisfied or become satisfied only when the chicken is exactly aligned with a stationary car. Since the chickens can pass through immobilized cars without penalty (the simplification states that the chicken can cross without timing gaps), those conditions rarely fire, and the policy’s default path through the nested `if` statements leads to the **UP** action almost every tick. As a result, the chicken steadily moves upward with little or no backward motion, quickly traverses all ten lanes, reaches the top, and then (because the task resets the chicken to the bottom after each successful crossing) repeats the same rapid ascent. The agent therefore achieves a very high score, crossing the freeway many more times than in the baseline.
