1. Baseline behavior in the original environment.
The policy acts as a dynamic, proximity-based collision avoidance controller. It continuously evaluates the Euclidean distances and relative coordinates between the chicken and surrounding cars. When nearby vehicles are far enough away (distances exceed the learned thresholds), it outputs UP to advance across lanes. When a car approaches within a critical distance, it switches to DOWN or NOOP to retreat or wait, effectively timing its movements to exploit gaps in the moving traffic. This enables the chicken to repeatedly cross the ten lanes, avoid collisions, and accumulate points.

2. Behavior in the simplified environment.
With all cars completely immobilized, the dynamic threat is removed. The policy will predominantly output UP, as the distance-based conditions that trigger DOWN or NOOP are either not met or quickly resolved by moving past the static vehicles. The chicken will cross the road rapidly and repeatedly without needing to wait for traffic gaps. It may occasionally step back or pause if it approaches a stationary car too closely, but it will not get stuck in timing loops and will efficiently traverse the lanes to maximize its score.
