The policy is a rule‑based controller that mainly tries to keep the player’s paddle vertically aligned with the ball.  

* **When the paddle is above the ball** (`LT(Player1,Ball1).y ≤ 6`) it checks the enemy paddle’s height and the horizontal distance of the ball from the enemy.  
  * If the ball is moving left toward the enemy (`D(Ball1,Enemy1).x ≤ -59`) it makes fine vertical adjustments:  
    – If the vertical gap is very small (`D(Player1,Ball1).y ≤ -4`) it either does nothing or presses **FIRE** (serve) depending on the enemy‑ball distance.  
    – If the gap is larger it moves the paddle **DOWN** (`RIGHT`) or **UP** (`LEFT`) and sometimes adds **FIRE** (`RIGHTFIRE`/`LEFTFIRE`) to serve while moving.  
  * If the ball is not heading left, it looks at the vertical distance to the enemy and either moves the paddle toward the ball (`LEFT`/`RIGHT`) or does nothing, occasionally firing when the enemy is far away.  

* **When the paddle is below the ball** (`LT(Player1,Ball1).y > 6`) the same logic is applied with the actions mirrored: the agent moves the paddle **UP** (`LEFT`) or **DOWN** (`RIGHT`) to close the vertical gap, and adds **FIRE** when the ball is far vertically or the enemy paddle is low, otherwise it holds position (`NOOP`).  

In short, the policy continuously drives the paddle up or down to track the ball’s y‑position, and it presses the fire button (serve) after a point or when the ball is out of easy reach, resulting in a predictive paddle‑tracking behavior that returns the ball more often than it misses.
