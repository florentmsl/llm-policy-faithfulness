The policy continuously decides how to move the player’s paddle (or when to serve) based on the ball’s position and the paddle’s current state.

* **When the ball is near the top of the screen** (`Ball1.y ≤ 35`):
  * If the paddle is vertically close to the ball (`C(Player1,Ball1).y ≤ 56`) and the overall distance to the ball is small (`ED(Player1,Ball1) ≤ 110`), the agent **fires** (serves) the ball.
  * If the paddle is close but farther away, it moves the paddle **left** (which, in Pong, corresponds to moving the paddle upward) to intercept the ball.
  * If the paddle is a bit farther vertically (`56 < C ≤ 79`):
    * When the paddle is already moving upward quickly (`DV(Player1).y ≤ -8`), it does nothing (`NOOP`).
    * Otherwise, if the paddle is not too low (`Player1.y[t‑1] ≤ 144`) or its horizontal speed is low (`V(Player1).x ≤ 2`), it again moves the paddle left (up); if neither condition holds, it waits.
  * If the paddle is far above the ball (`C > 79`), it waits (`NOOP`).

* **When the ball is lower on the screen** (`Ball1.y > 35`):
  * If the ball’s recent vertical position is still relatively high (`Ball1.y#1 ≤ 59`):
    * When the ball is on the left half of the screen (`Ball1.x ≤ 89`), the agent fires (serves).
    * Otherwise it moves the paddle **right** (downward) to meet the ball.
  * If the ball is low (`Ball1.y#1 > 59`), the agent consistently moves the paddle right (down).

In short, the policy tries to **serve or move the paddle upward** when the ball is high and near the paddle, and to **move the paddle downward** when the ball is lower, thereby attempting to return the ball past the opponent as often as possible. The `LEFT` action corresponds to upward paddle motion, `RIGHT` to downward motion, and `FIRE` to serving the ball when the situation is favorable.
