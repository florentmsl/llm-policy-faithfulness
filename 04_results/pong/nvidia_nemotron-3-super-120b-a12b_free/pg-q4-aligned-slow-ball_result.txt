**Baseline behavior (original ball speed)**  
The policy constantly checks the ball’s vertical offset relative to the paddle (using `D(Player1,Ball1).y`, `LT` comparisons, etc.) and issues a move‑up (`LEFT`) or move‑down (`RIGHT`) action when the ball is noticeably above or below the paddle. When the vertical offset is small it mostly returns `NOOP` or a serve‑only action (`FIRE`). The policy also contains branches that fire (`LEFTFIRE`/`RIGHTFIRE`) when the paddle is aligned with the ball and a serve is needed. Overall, the agent tries to keep its paddle near the ball’s y‑position and to serve after a point, but because the ball travels at the original speed the paddle sometimes arrives too late, leading to occasional missed hits and points for the opponent.

**Behavior in the simplified setup (ball speed reduced by 35 %)**  
The same decision tree is executed, but the ball now moves more slowly toward the paddle while the paddle’s maximum speed is unchanged. Consequently, the conditions that trigger `LEFT` or `RIGHT` moves are satisfied earlier, giving the paddle more time to reach the ball’s y‑position. The agent therefore makes contact with the ball more frequently, serves correctly after points, and misses far fewer shots. The expected outcome is a higher return‑rate and a greater likelihood of scoring more points than the opponent compared with the baseline.
