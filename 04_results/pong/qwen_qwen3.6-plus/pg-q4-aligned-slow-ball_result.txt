1. Baseline behavior in the original environment.
The policy implements a threshold-based vertical tracking strategy. It continuously evaluates the vertical offset between the player paddle and the ball (using features like `D(Player1, Ball1).y` and `LT(Player1, Ball1).y`) and issues LEFT or RIGHT commands to align the paddle with the ball’s y-coordinate. It also conditions its responses on the ball’s horizontal position (`Ball1.x`), vertical velocity (`DV(Ball1).y`), and distance to the opponent to time its movements. During rallies, it reliably intercepts and returns the ball, using FIRE primarily for serving. It performs competently, though occasional misses may occur when the ball’s speed or trajectory exceeds the paddle’s reaction window within the policy’s fixed thresholds.

2. Behavior in the simplified environment.
With ball speed reduced by 35% and paddle speed unchanged, the policy operates with a significantly larger temporal margin. The same distance and velocity thresholds are met more gradually, allowing the paddle to correct vertical misalignments more easily and with less urgency. The agent will track the ball more smoothly, return a higher percentage of shots, and maintain or improve its win rate. Since the policy relies on relative positioning rather than high-speed reflexes, the slower ball dynamics only enhance its tracking effectiveness without altering its core decision logic.
